    DGM Workshop on data exchange through WIS (DGM-WMO, 17-18 January 2022)

    Scope of the workshop

    As part of the capacity building of National Meteorological and Hydrological Services (NMHSs) of Central and West African countries, and under the aegis of the project “West Africa Region: Seamless Operational Forecast Systems and Technical Assistance for Capacity Building (CREWS-West Africa) ", funded by the CREWS initiative (Climate Risk and Early Warning Systems), the General Directorate of Meteorology (DGM) of Morocco , in collaboration with the World Meteorological Organization (WMO), are organizing a series of training workshops on:

    Data exchange through WIS.

    17 and 18 January, 2022.

    These workshops will take place online via the Zoom platform (see GUIDE), a link will be sent to registered participants in due course.

    The main objectives of these workshops are:

    • 1. Introduire WMO Information System(WIS),
    • 2. Casablanca's GISC (Global Information Sytem Center) roles and responsibilities,
    • 3. GISC Casablanca's interaction with RA-I NCs and DCPCs,
    • 4. Benefits of data exchange,
    • 5. Presenting WIGOS Data Quality Monitoring System (WDQMS) webtool
    • 6. Providing a summary view of the new WIS strategy (WIS 2.0)

    The main topics to be covered are:

    • 1. Introducing WMO information system in its current form,
    • 2. Roles and responsibilities of a GISC center in WIS
    • 3. Interaction wetween GISC, NC and DCPC,
    • 4. Data exchange through WIS : Why ?,
    • 5. Data exchange monitoring tool(WDQMS).

    Participants and workshop schedule

    The target countries of the workshops are countries of West and Central Africa, including the 24 members of PRESASS, of PRESAGG and PRESAC. The content of these 2 workshops is the same. The working language used for the first workshop will be French and for the second workshop it will be English.
